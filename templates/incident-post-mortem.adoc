=== Root Cause

Describe the technical root cause clearly. Distinguish between:

* *Trigger:* The immediate event that caused the incident
* *Underlying cause:* The systemic condition that allowed the trigger to cause an outage
* *Contributing factors:* Conditions that made detection slower or impact worse

_Most incidents have a trigger that's easy to identify and an underlying cause
that's harder to admit. The value is in the underlying cause._

== Guidance for Writing Post-Mortems

=== The Purpose

A post-mortem exists to make the system and the team better. It is not an
investigation into who made a mistake. If people are afraid of the post-mortem,
they will hide information — and the next incident will be worse.

=== Blameless Does Not Mean Accountable-less

Blameless means we don't punish people for honest mistakes or for being
the person closest to the failure. It doesn't mean we avoid identifying
what went wrong. Systems fail because of systemic conditions, not because
individuals are careless.

=== Specificity Over Thoroughness

A post-mortem with three specific, actionable findings is worth more than
one with twenty vague observations. Focus on what you can change.

=== The 24/48 Rule

Write the timeline within 24 hours while memories are fresh. Complete
the analysis within 48 hours while the team's attention is still focused.
Post-mortems written weeks later are archaeology, not learning.

=== Follow Through

A post-mortem without completed action items is a document. A post-mortem
with completed action items is an improvement. Track them, review them,
and close them.
