# Engineering Fieldnotes

Practical material from 30 years of designing, building, and occasionally inheriting software systems.

Not tutorials. Not hot takes. Just the things I've found worth writing down — because I've seen the same problems enough times to think a structured prompt is more useful than another opinion piece.

Most of it is aimed at engineers and architects working on systems that have grown beyond their original design. That's where the interesting problems tend to live.

## About

I'm Ian Robinson — a Software Architect and Consultant based in the UK.

I've spent most of my career at the intersection of architecture and delivery: designing systems that need to scale, reviewing ones that are struggling to, and working with teams navigating the gap between where their software is and where the business needs it to go.

The material here reflects what I actually think, based on what I've actually seen. If something is wrong or could be better, I'm genuinely open to hearing it.
