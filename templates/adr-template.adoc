=== Decision

State the decision clearly. One or two sentences.

=== Rationale

Explain _why_ this option was chosen over the alternatives. Reference the context
and constraints that made this the best available choice — not the ideal one.

_The rationale is the most important section of an ADR. Six months from now,
nobody will question what you decided — they'll question why._

=== Consequences

What changes as a result of this decision?

* *Positive:* What does this enable or improve?
* *Negative:* What cost, risk, or limitation does this introduce?
* *Neutral:* What other decisions or changes does this require?

== Usage Notes

=== File Naming

Use sequential numbering: `adr-001-use-postgresql.adoc`, `adr-002-adopt-event-sourcing.adoc`.

=== Principles

* *Immutable records:* Don't edit accepted ADRs. If a decision changes, write a new
ADR that supersedes the old one and update the old one's status.
* *Short and honest:* An ADR that takes more than 15 minutes to write is trying to
do too much. If the decision is genuinely complex, break it into smaller decisions.
* *Record what you rejected:* The options you didn't choose are as valuable as the
one you did. Future engineers will face similar tradeoffs and need to understand
why alternatives were ruled out.
* *Include the uncomfortable parts:* If the decision has known downsides, say so.
An ADR that only lists positives isn't a decision record — it's a sales pitch.

=== When to Write One

Write an ADR when a decision:

* Is difficult to reverse
* Affects multiple teams or services
* Involves a significant tradeoff
* Will be questioned by future engineers
* Changes a pattern the team currently follows

Don't write one for routine choices that follow established patterns.
